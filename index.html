‎

Exploring consciousness-based healing through philosophical, historical, scientific, and ethical lenses

‎

By Syed Waleed Mehboob Ali

‎
‎ ‎ ‎

Introduction

‎
‎ ‎

The author of this paper, Syed Waleed Mehboob Ali is a businessman operating in the construction and marble sector. Outside of his commercial responsibilities, he has invested years in researching and practicing a consciousness-based healing method. Rooted in both experiential observation and intellectual synthesis, this method does not emerge from religious mysticism but from a rational inquiry into the relationship between human cognition, subtle energy, and behavior. This paper introduces and defends the concept of 'Divine Energy' as a subtle but powerful presence in the human experience, explored through philosophical, historical, scientific, and ethical lenses.

What is Divine Energy?

‎
‎
‎
‎

Divine energy refers to a higher-dimensional form of conscious energy that permeates existence and interacts subtly with the human mind-body system. It is not electricity, magnetism, or any commonly understood physical force, it is a metaphysical phenomenon that functions at the intersection of consciousness, intention, and universal laws.

‎
‎
‎ ‎
‎
‎ ‎

Philosophical Points:

‎
    ‎
  • Unity of Existence: The ancient philosophical idea that all creation is interconnected and emerges from a singular source aligns with the concept of divine energy.
    • ‎
  • Conscious Intent as Force: Many spiritual philosophies treat intention as a force, divine energy may be the medium through which such intentions operate.
    • ‎
  • Haroot-Maroot Knowledge: Ancient traditions (as hinted in stories like Haroot and Maroot) propose the existence of an advanced knowledge system accessible to certain individuals. This knowledge includes manipulating unseen realities ethically.

Ancient Civilizations

  • The proliferation of consciousness-related fields like noetic sciences and neuroplasticity has reopened doors to understanding divine energy in scientific language.

Conclusion

‎
‎ ‎

Divine energy, as understood in ancient traditions and being rediscovered in the age of technology, represents a conscious force operating beyond the physical realm. It is accessible to those with a natural inclination, deep self-awareness, and ethical discipline. This energy is not a myth, it is a hidden framework through which subtle influence, healing, and alignment occur. In the Age of WiFi, as frequencies surround and shape us more than ever, reclaiming inner energetic clarity becomes not only possible but essential.

‎

The author proposes this not as an absolute doctrine but as an evolving field of inquiry that merges the sacred with the scientific. While not yet measurable by standard instruments, divine energy manifests in human transformation, intention, and synchronicity. It is a gift, a test, and a tool.

Frequently Asked Questions

‎
‎
‎
Is this energy the same as spirituality or religion?
‎

No. Divine energy is a metaphysical concept that can align with, but is not limited to religious frameworks. It is more aligned with consciousness-based science.

‎
‎ ‎
‎
Is there any proof this energy exists?
‎

There is growing indirect evidence through frequency science, noetic studies, quantum models, and brain wave research. But direct physical evidence is still evolving.

‎
‎ ‎
‎
Can everyone access this energy?
‎

Not equally. Some are born with a natural affinity. Others may train and develop partial access, but mastery requires a specific internal configuration.

‎
‎ ‎
‎
Is this the same as black magic or sorcery?
‎

No. Black magic relies on rituals and often unethical intent. Divine energy operates through inner alignment and moral clarity.

‎
‎ ‎
‎
What is meant by Haroot and Maroot knowledge?
‎

A symbolic representation of knowledge sent by God that could be used for good or evil. The author's concept parallels this as a consciousness-based science requiring ethical grounding.

‎
‎ ‎
‎
Can this energy cause harm?
‎

Any powerful tool can. Divine energy must be wielded ethically. The internal ethical compass of the practitioner determines the outcome.

‎
‎ ‎
‎
Why 'In the Age of WiFi'?
‎

Because modern humanity is deeply affected by frequency-driven environments. The term represents a need to understand divine energy through today's lens of connectivity, cognition, and complexity.
